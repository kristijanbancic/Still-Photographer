About Kristijan

Kristijan is a Still Photographer

Kristijan Bančić is a Croatian still photographer from Rovinj. His passion for photography began in high school, where he seriously encountered and chose a photographic direction that laid the foundation for his creative path. Shortly afterward, he enrolled in graphic design in Zagreb and worked as an intern in the visual assets field, creating layouts and logos for various media platforms. He later graduated with the highest honors and grade of excellence in photography from the Faculty of Applied Sciences in Ljubljana (Slovenia), where he analyzed and focused his thesis on CMOS, CCD and other sensor technologies - advantages and disadvantages. Kristijan is best known for his work as a unit still photographer on Ekho 2011, as well as numerous experimental and student film projects. He has also worked as a behind-the-scenes videographer, photographic archivist-negative asset manager, and reprographic photographer used for official catalogs and archives in the Museum City of Rovinj. His work has been exhibited at several exhibitions: in Cyprus, Germany, Macedonia, Slovenia, and Croatia. He was awarded a FIAP Diploma for Croatia 2011 in the Open Color section for his conceptual image Distorted Reality and received first prize in the FOTO3 young authors’ category. In 2016, he won another first-place award for a photo story from Rovinj featuring a well-known local fisherman. His photographs has also been published in several newspapers highlighting both his artistic perspective and documentary approach.

Kristijan has been an active and proud member of the Center of Visual Arts (CVA-Batana) in Rovinj since 2011. He considers and sees himself an eternal explorer of film and photography, always seeking new ways to experiment with visual storytelling. In his free time, he enjoys creating abstract photographs that blur the lines between reality and imagination.
